About my work

In October 2021 I joined the team of “Dragon City''. This project, originally released in 2012 for Facebook, is Socialpoint’s flagship title and has delighted millions of players during its 10 years of existence. More importantly, the game stays strong and has ambitious plans for its future.

My role is being the game designer for one of the two development squads, providing ideation, documentation and implementation of several new features. All under supervision of the lead designer and product leads. But a project like this also involves the maintenance of old systems and I tweaked and updated different systems created many years ago in a true exercise in digital paleontology.

Working on a game like this entails its own challenges, whether it is technical debt, the quirks of “authoritative backend” or keeping the frail chain of knowledge as the team members invariably rotate over time. Also, being a successful game with over 1.5 millions DAU, every change in the game is carefully discussed and A/B tested, making things move slowly but steadily.

And, while I will never claim this to be a strength of mine, I’m in the right environment to improve my product sensitivity. This means measuring new features in terms of business return, or justifying design proposals through data. These are areas difficult to develop while in smaller games or prototypes as opposed to DC which is a consolidated business.

Despite the multiple challenges, working for “Dragon City” is also very rewarding because your actions have an impact on millions of players who care deeply about the game and that is what every game designer strives for.

  • Improved my content management skills by learning from the game’s ancient spreadsheets crafted and refined by generations of game designers.
